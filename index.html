Academic Background

Master of Applied Science, Optimization

Thesis: A Submodular Representation for Network Design Problems (Supervisor: Dr. Ivan Contreras)

Study Focus: Concentration on the single Assignment hub location problem

  • Combinatorial Optimization: Application of combinatorial optimization techniques to develop a framework maximizing the efficiency of the hub network under a single allocation constraint.
  • Submodularity Exploration: Detailed exploration of the submodularity property in the framework's objective function, a property vital for the efficient solution of combinatorial problems.
  • Optimization Framework: Development of a combinatorial framework that embraces submodularity, enabling robust and scalable solution strategies.
  • Algorithm Development: Creation of an efficient polynomial-time approximation algorithm tailored to the submodular framework, focusing on computational feasibility and accuracy.
  • Theoretical Contributions: Rigorous theoretical analysis validating the algorithm's conformity to performance bounds, ensuring reliability under theoretical worst-case scenarios.
